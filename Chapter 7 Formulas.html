Master Formulas

Step 1: Find Contribution Margin

Contribution Margin = Sales Revenues - Variable Costs

Step 2: Find Relevant Net Income

Relevant Net Income = Contribution Margin - Avoidable Fixed Costs
Combined Formula:
Relevant Net Income = Sales Revenues - Variable Costs - Avoidable Fixed Costs

Variable Dictionary

Sales Revenues

The total amount of money generated by selling products or services within this specific segment. It's the starting point for profitability.

Variable Costs

Costs that change directly in proportion to your sales volume (e.g., direct materials, direct labor, shipping). This calculator lets you enter this as a straight dollar amount OR a percentage of sales.

Avoidable Fixed Costs

Fixed costs that would disappear if you decided to shut down or drop this segment (e.g., the segment manager's salary, advertising specific to this product). These are highly relevant to segment decisions.

Un-avoidable Fixed Costs

General company costs allocated to this segment that will continue regardless of whether you keep or drop the segment (e.g., CEO's salary, general factory rent). We ignore these when calculating "Relevant" Net Income because they don't change your decision.
